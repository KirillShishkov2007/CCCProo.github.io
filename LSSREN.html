What is LSSR

LSSR

Lithuanian Soviet Socialist Republic (lit. Lietuvos Tarybų Socialistinė Respublika, Lietuvos TSR) (LSSR, Soviet Lithuania) is a union Soviet socialist republic. One of the republics of the USSR in the period 1940-1990, the territory and borders of which since 1946 coincided with the territory and borders of the present Republic of Lithuania (not taking into account only minor changes in the borders with Belarus). The LSSR was located in the west of the European part of the USSR, bordering in the north with the Latvian SSR, in the south and east with the Byelorussian SSR, in the southwest with the Kaliningrad region of the RSFSR and Poland. In the west it was washed by the Baltic Sea. Area - 65.2 thousand km². The capital is Vilnius. Was the only republic in the USSR with a predominantly Catholic population. On March 11, 1990, the Supreme Soviet of the LSSR issued a law on renaming the state into the Republic of Lithuania and proclaimed an act on the restoration of Lithuania's independence. On September 6, 1991, the State Council of the USSR recognized the independence of Lithuania.
